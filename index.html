Bhavya Balu

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Chemical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Prof. Aditya Khair. I work on mathematical modeling of ion transport phenomena in electrochemical and electrokinetic systems, with a specific interest in quantifying the effects of ion asymmetry. I obtained my Bachelor's degree from the Indian Institute of Technology Madras, where I worked with Prof. Raghunathan Rengaswamy on modeling droplet behavior in 2D microchannels.

 

Research

My research focuses on the mesoscale modeling of ion transport in electrochemical and colloidal systems under time-dependent, externally applied voltages, to predict macroscopic, measurable quantities, such as the charging time scale, the motion of charged particles, or the electrical interaction between surfaces in electrolyte. Electrolytes under time-dependent voltages have applications in energy storage, microfluidic pumping devices, de-ionization through porous membranes, and separation and assembly of particles and macromolecules. Additionally, the interaction between colloidal particles in electrolyte indicates the stability of the colloidal formulation. My specific interest has been to quantify the effect of asymmetry in the diffusion coefficients of the ions on its transport, and hence, the measurable properties of the system. I primarily utilize mathematical methods such as perturbation analysis and integral transforms to obtain asymptotic solutions to the system of differential equations that govern ion transport in these systems.
